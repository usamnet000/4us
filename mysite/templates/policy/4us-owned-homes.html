Frequently asked questions

How are Zillow-owned homes different from others?

Zillow-owned homes are move-in ready homes from a seller you trust. You’ll enjoy your new place on day one, since every Zillow-owned home features fresh updates throughout.

Is Zillow willing to make repairs?

Zillow-owned homes are move-in ready and have been professionally renovated or repaired as needed.

Additionally, every buyer is encouraged to hire a licensed home inspector to assess the home and identify any issues before moving forward with the offer.

Following a home inspection by the buyer’s licensed inspector, Zillow may make repairs, give credits in lieu of repairs or decline repairs. As with any home sale transaction, this is reviewed case by case.

What if there’s something wrong with the systems or structure of the home?

All repairs covered by Zillow are assessed on a case-by-case basis. Prior to purchasing the home, Zillow performs an evaluation looking specifically for structural and system issues. Should an issue arise, contact the property’s listing agent.

How are agents involved when Zillow sells a home it owns?

We use licensed agents and brokers to sell the home and pay commission to both the seller’s agent and the buyer’s agent, much like an individual seller.

Can I use my own buyer’s agent?

Yes, you are welcome to use an agent of your choice. Zillow believes that all transactions should have agent representation.

Why is the Zestimate different from the sale price of a Zillow-owned home?

The Zestimate is a computer-generated estimate of a home’s value based on millions of data points, including public records information, basic home facts and market trends. The Zestimate is not an appraisal, and you can’t use it in place of an appraisal, though you can certainly share it with real estate professionals. It’s a starting point, but it can’t “see” inside a home. To determine the sale price for a Zillow-owned home, we consider the Zestimate, an in-person assessment, a local real estate agent’s opinion and any increased value from renovations or improvements to the home. Because the sale price accounts for these additional factors, it may be different than the Zestimate.

How do I know you’re not making the Zestimate lower so you can buy homes for less or higher so you can sell them for more?

The Zestimate is a trusted tool for consumers, and we take its credibility very seriously. Factors that can influence the Zestimate include homeowner updates to their home facts, the sale prices of recently sold nearby homes, changes in tax-assessed values, and other updates or additions to independent public data related to the home. The fact that Zillow owns and is selling a home will not change the Zestimate.

How can I see the status of my offer?

Once you’ve made an offer, either you or your buyer’s agent can reach out to our listing agent for updates. You can expect a response to your offer within approximately 48 hours if submitted during the week or 72 hours if submitted on Friday or Saturday.

Why is Zillow buying homes?

Zillow’s goal has always been to empower buyers and sellers by providing information and resources around the real estate process. Zillow Offers supports this goal by helping sellers make confident, informed decisions about their home sale, and if they sell to Zillow, we can provide a professional, convenient and personalized experience without the hassle of multiple showings and unpredictable timing. For homes that don’t qualify for offers, we will continue to connect sellers with a local agent who can help them list their home on the open market.
